# HR Employee Attrition Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition using Power BI. The dashboard provides interactive visualizations and key performance indicators (KPIs) to help organizations understand why employees leave and identify factors that impact employee retention.

The dashboard was built using the HR Employee Attrition dataset and includes various analytical views such as department-wise attrition, job role analysis, age group analysis, overtime impact, job satisfaction, and work-life balance assessment.

## Objectives

* Analyze employee attrition patterns.
* Identify departments with the highest turnover.
* Evaluate the impact of job satisfaction and work-life balance on attrition.
* Examine attrition across different age groups and job roles.
* Provide actionable insights to improve employee retention.

## Key Features

* Total Employees KPI
* Employees Left KPI
* Attrition Rate KPI
* Average Salary KPI
* Attrition by Department
* Attrition Rate by Department
* Attrition by Age Group
* Attrition by Job Role
* Attrition by Job Satisfaction
* Attrition by Work-Life Balance
* Attrition by OverTime
* Interactive Filters and Slicers
* Business Insights Section

## Tools Used

* Power BI Desktop
* DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
* Data Visualization
* Data Modeling
* Business Intelligence

## Key Insights

* Employees aged 26–35 have the highest attrition.
* Research & Development and Sales departments experience the highest employee turnover.
* Employees working overtime are more likely to leave the organization.
* Lower job satisfaction and poor work-life balance are associated with higher attrition rates.
* Attrition patterns vary across departments and job roles.

## Business Impact

The dashboard enables HR professionals and management teams to monitor workforce trends, identify high-risk employee groups, and develop effective retention strategies through data-driven decision-making.
